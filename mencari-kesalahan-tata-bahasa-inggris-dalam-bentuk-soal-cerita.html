God appeared at the spot and said, "See, how you are killing other innocent ants to kill the one which bit you. You were blaming me for my unjust act. Look at your own actions before blaming me for anything."

The king of Prambanan had a daughter named Roro Jonggarang. She was very beautiful. Unfortunely, Bandung Bondowoso fell in love with her beauty. He really wanted to marry Jonggrang.
“You are very attractive, do you want to be my lady?” asked Bandung Bondowoso.

Jonggarang was surprised and shocked with his request. She didn’t want to marry a man like Bondowoso. She wanted to say no, but she was afraid that Bondowoso would be mad and angry. She was also afraid that he would endangered her people of Prambanan. Then, she had a plan.

“If you really want to marry me, I give you one night to construct a thousand temples.” Said Jonggrang.
“That is useless! I can’t build it in one night!” said Bondowoso. But, Bondowoso did not give up. He came to his adviser.
“Oh my king, you can asked your genies to help you with her request.” Said his adviser.

At the same time, Jonggrang heard from her assistant that the temple construction was almost finished. She was so anxious. The, she had another great idea. She called every servant to help her.
“Please help me to prepare a lot of straws and mortars.” Asked Jonggrang. “Burn the straws and make some noise from the mortars.”
All of her servants did what she asked. The sound of mortars and the burning straw fooled the genies. They thought the sun was going to rise.

Bondowoso could not stop the genies from leaving him. He was mad. He knew that Jonggrang had tricked him.
“You can’t trick me, Jonggrang. I have already made 999 temples. I just need to construct one more temple!”

The Greedy Boy

There lived a twin brother called Joko and Joni. They were an identical twins, even their mother sometimes found it too hard to differentiate them. Although they were a twins, they were very different in everything when it comes to their appearance. They never agreed with anything.

Joko had no friends, whereas for Joni the world was friendship. Joko loved sweet foods and Joni loved to eat spicy foods. Joko loved his mother so much and Joni loved his father more. While Joko was generous, Joni was greedy.

While they grew up, their father called them to talk about something. Their father wanted to share his wealth equally to them. Joni did not agree with the agreement. He argued that he is more strong and intelligent than Joko. He asked for higher wealth to his father.

Then, their father arranged a competition between them to decided who could get the higher wealth. He ordered his twin sons to walk as far as they can and they had to return home before the sunset. Whoever walked the longer distance and returned home on time would get huge amount of his wealth proportionate to distance they walked. Their father did not give the watch to calculate time.

Joko and Joni started to walk during a sunny day. Joko walked slowly but steadily, while Joni started to run as fas as he could.

Joko predicted that the distance he cover until mid-noon must be equal to the distance he would reach house before sunset. When mid-noon came, Joko decided to return back so he could back home on time.

However, Joni, with his greediness to earn more wealth, did not stop running even after mid-noon. He ran two times longer than the distance Joko was covered. Then, he decided to return back to home. He ran as fast as he could but the Sun had already turned orange. Unfortunately, he could not return to home on time. The sun had already set in his half way to home.

Joni was defeated because of his greediness!

Moral Value : GREEDINESS WILL MAKE YOU LOSE EVERYTHING
