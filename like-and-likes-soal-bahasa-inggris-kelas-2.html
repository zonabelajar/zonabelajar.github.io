2. Apa bedanya like dan likes bahasa inggris


Like, sebagai kata kerja, berarti suka/menyukai.

Penggunaan like atau likes tergantung pada subject-nya. Bila subject tunggal, menggunakan likes. Bila subject jamak, menggunakan like.

I, you, they, we = like

she, he, it, name = likes

contoh dalam kalimat

1. I like to eat fried chicken

2. She likes to drink orange juice.

3. We like to play football.

4. My father likes to read magazine

5. The students like to study in the library.

6. Everyone likes to sing that song together.

Everyone, meski artinya setiap orang tetapi tetap dianggap tunggal, karena everyone merupakan kata ganti (pronoun).

6. bagaimana cara penempatan like and likes


tergantung dari subjeknya. kalau subjeknya i, you, they, we menggunakan like. kalau subjeknya he, she, it menggunakan likes.
(untuk penggunaan pada simple present tense)Semua tergantung subjek

Like : I, You, They, We
Likes : He, She, It

Tapi, kalau ada "modals", "likes" (yang menggunakan S tidak berlaku lagi), modals itu -> will, can, may, dll.

contoh :
1) He will like it.
2) He likes it

di nomor 1, walaupun subjeknya "he", tapi pakainya like (tanpa S), karena ada modal (will) sebelum kata like (verb)

Jawaban dari siswa SD6 juga, penjelasan ada dibawah

Examples :

Alice reads a book. (third person singular)

I read a book. (first person singular)

You read a book. (second person singular)

Adding "s" to a verb for one person is a part of the conjugation rules in English, specifically for the third person singular. It's a way to match the verb form with the subject in the present tense. This rule helps indicate the subject's number (singular) and person (third person) in the sentence. It's a fundamental aspect of English grammar.

The use of "doesn't" instead of "don't" depends on whether the subject is singular or plural.

1. "Doesn't" is used with a singular subject, including a name:

- Alice doesn't like chocolate.

2. "Don't" is used with plural subjects:

- They don't like chocolate.

The choice between "doesn't" and "don't" is determined by the number (singular or plural) of the subject. In the case of a name like "Alice," it's a singular subject, so you use "doesn't." If you're talking about multiple people or things, you would use "don't" with a plural subject.

Note :

First person singular = I

Second person singular = You

Third person singular = He/She/It

First person plural = We

Second person plural = You

Third person plural = They

(I also is included into plural when it comes to grammar)
