7. contoh narative text pendek serta artinya


Flock of Doves
Once upon a time, there was a flock of doves that flew in search of food led by their king. They had flown a long distance and were very tired. The smallest dove picked up speed and found some rice scattered beneath a banyan tree. So all the doves landed and began to eat.
Suddenly a net fell over them and they were all trapped. They saw a hunter approaching carrying. The doves desperately fluttered their wings trying to get out, but to no avail. The king had an idea. He advised all the doves to fly up together carrying the net with them.
Each dove picked up a portion of the net and together they flew off carrying the net with them. The hunter looked up in astonishment. He tried to follow them, but they were flying high over hills and valleys. They flew to a hill near a city of temples where there lived a mouse who could help them. He was a faithful friend of the dove king.
The mouse agreed saying that he would set the king free first. The king insisted that he first free his subjects and the king last. The mouse understood the king’s feelings and complied with his wishes. He began to cut the net and one by one all the doves were freed including the dove king.

             Once upon a time, in a village near the jungle, there were two poor best friends named Cleo and Patra. Every day, they went to the jungle to find fire wood to be sold. Cleo was  strong while Patra was quick and agile. Cleo could fell down a tree with one punch while Patra was able to cut the tree to small piece in very short time. They always helped each other and they get along together, and they were afraid of nothing.

One day they found a treasure map in the jungle. They were so surprised and Cleo invited Patra to find out the treasure.

“Patra look I got something such a treasure map” Cleo whispered.

“It just only a fake map, buddy. Don’t trust it” Patra Answered.

“You always think too short”. Cleo replied. “How about if we try to find it out tomorrow”? He invited.

“Hmmmm” Patra was thinking.

“If we are lucky we will be the richest men in this village. Like what we always dream” Patra said.

Okay. Patra replied.

. The next day Cleo and Patra went to find the treasure based on the map they had gotten. The map guided them to a cave. Then they went inside. After they had walked for hundred meters, they met two branches of tunnels -yellow and blue tunnel- in the cave. As their problem, there was not a direction about what the right tunnel in the map, so that they were confused to think which tunnel they should choose.

As a result, they began to debate. Cleo thought that blue tunnel was the right way, whereas Patra preferred to choose Yellow cave. Then they were insulting each other.

“Blue tunnel is the right way” Cleo said.

“But I have good felling with this yellow tunnel” Patra replied.

Then Cleo said “Your felling is hoax, just listen to me. I always better making decision than you”.

 Patra was very angry hearing Cleo’s last statement and they fought. Finally they separated by choosing different tunnel to get the treasure. After that, each of them met big and spooky creatures who guarded the treasure. They were very afraid and felt so weak, so they run back while the creatures run after them until Cleo and Patra met again at the beginning of those tunnels.

  After they had met again, they felt to be strong again and together, they could defeat those creatures easily. Finally, they realized that they should not fight, because both of them had excesses and weaknesses each other and they could be strong together. Finally, they asked for sorry each other and be friend again while they became the richest men in their village since got the treasure.


20. berikan contoh text narative bahasa iggris beserta struktur teksnya


Once, a cap seller was passing through a jungle. He was very tired and needed to rest. Then, he stopped and spread a cloth under a tree. He placed his bag full of caps near him and lay down with his cap on his head.
The cap seller had a sound sleep for one hour. When he got up, the first thing he did was to look into his bag. He was startled when he found all his caps were not there.
When he looked up the sky, he was very surprised to see monkeys sitting on the branches of a tree, each of the monkeys is wearing a cap of on its head. They had evidently done it to imitate him
He decided to get his caps back by making a humble request to the monkeys. In return, the monkeys only made faces of him. When he begun to make gesture, the monkeys also imitated him.
At last he found a clever idea. ” Monkeys are a great imitator,” he thought. So he took off his own cap and threw it down on the ground. And as he had expected, all the monkeys took off the caps and threw the caps down on the ground. Quickly, he stood up and collected the caps, put them back into his bag and went away.

Note on Generic Structure: The generic structure of above narrative story is:
Orientation: The cap seller as the participant (main character), once time as time setting, and in the jungle as place setting. We note from discussion that the elements of orientation is shortly answering who, when and where of the story.
Complication: The cap seller wants the monkeys to bring back his caps but the monkeys do not return them. Of course this makes the cap seller face a problem. Complication in a narrative text is not always in single problem. That is we know a major conflict and minor conflict.
Resolution: The cap seller gets the monkey to bring back the cap by acting of throwing his own cap. The monkeys imitate what he has done so the problem is is solved. This story has happy ending as the cap seller has his caps returned.
