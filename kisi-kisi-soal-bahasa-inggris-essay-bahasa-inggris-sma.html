In our daily life, we often offering help to others. This is one of an important expression that we need to learn in order to be polite and helpful.

As a response of the offering we can accepting or rejecting the offers. Here we will learn more about how to accepting an offer or help.

Some phrases that we usually use when accepting an offer :

Yes, please, I'd like toYes, please that would be very helpfulIf you wouldn't mindThank you, I wouldThank you, that would be greatThank you, that's very kind of you

Here an example how to use those phrases in a dialog :

Dina : You look so sad today. What is happening? How can I help you? (Kamu kelihatan sedih hari ini. Apa yang terjadi?Apa yang bisa kubantu?)

Ari : I failed my math test today. (saya gagal dalam ujian matematika)

Dina : Don't worry, you still have one more chance for a retest, Would you like some help with your study? (Jangan khawatir, masih ada satu kesempatan untuk mengulang, apa kamu perlu bantuan dalam belajar?

Ari :  Yes please. That would be very helpful. (Ya, itu akan sangat membantu)

Dina : OK, let's study together tomorrow afternoon after school. (OK, kita bisa belajar bersama besok setelah sekolah)

Ari : That's great!. See you tomorrow afternoon then! (Ya, sampai ketemu besok!)

Dina : See you! (Sampai ketemu!)

News item is a text which informs readers about events of the day. The events are considered newsworthy or important.

Generic Structure of News Item

Main Events / Newsworthy event(s): it recounts the event in summary form. Elaboration / Background event(s): they elaborate what happened, to whom, in what circumstance Resource of Information (Source) : it contains comments by participants in, witnesses to and authorities expert on the event

Language Feature of News Item

Focusing on circumstances using a simple language in writing the text Using saying verbs: “….”, She said, He informed, We told, I reported. Sometimes at the beginning of news, the scene is mentioned (Places) : Jakarta – … .. / Bangkok – … ../Manila - .... Using Past tense in explaining news events. Usually the events are in the past But if it is a fact that until now still happen or still in the form of fact, then can use simple present tense. Short, telegraphic information about story captured in headline Using adverbs: time, place and manner. Uses of material processes to retell the event

Timeliness, in this element the meaning of the News is something new, what is happening or the current term "current", if likened to a food product, the news has a fast expiration time, information today could be stale the next morning, because the information must be on time.

2.Factual

Real or real, the content of this information is based on the events that actually occur, not the fabric of fiction, in other words the news must be in accordance with the actual conditions, in Journalism, facts consist of real events (opinions) and opinions (Opinion) news source (Statement).

3.
Important

In this case the news must contain content that is important so that the influence can be felt by the reader of this information, this element relates to the element of influence or impact, in the important value of the news apart from the influence on the reader. reporters held a Futsal match "this news was less important in the influence of the people but if there were famous figures, for example, who participated in the activity, the information was important" The President of the Republic of Indonesia held a Futsal match with reporters on Press Day.


4. Impact

These are very important characteristics, namely the influence, how the news influences the community and how seriously the community on the news can make benefits for the community, therefore, the content of the information submitted must be beneficial for the reader.

5.Conflict

Conflicts in the information, in this case conflict can make people interested in knowing one thing, starting from war, political, sports, or crime conflicts, the more interesting and interesting the conflict, the more interested the public will be to read it.
